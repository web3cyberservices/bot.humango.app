HUMANGO BOT AUDIT SCOPE & DETECTION LOGIC v1.0

Official documentation of technical vulnerabilities and compliance gaps monitored by the HumangoBot audit network.

1. GDPR & PRIVACY COMPLIANCE
- Missing Consent Management Platforms (CMP): Detection of absent cookie banners or consent overlays on pages using tracking technologies.
- Illegal Cookie Placement: Identification of non-essential cookies (marketing/analytics) set before explicit user consent.
- Legal Disclosure Validation (Impressum): Automated check for mandatory 'Impressum' or 'Legal Notice' sections (specifically for EU domains .de, .at, .fr, .it).
- Unsecure Data Transmission: Identifying forms (<form>) transmitting data over unencrypted HTTP instead of HTTPS (Art. 32 GDPR).
- PII Exposure Risk: Heuristic detection of potential personal data exposure in public technical logs or directory listings.

2. SECURITY & INFRASTRUCTURE
- Missing Security Headers: Verification of 'Content-Security-Policy' (CSP), 'X-Frame-Options' (Clickjacking protection), and 'Strict-Transport-Security' (HSTS).
- Transport Layer Security: Assessment of SSL/TLS certificate validity and the use of deprecated protocols (e.g., TLS 1.0/1.1).
- Client-Side Vulnerabilities: Signature-based detection of outdated or known-vulnerable JavaScript libraries and frameworks.

3. ACCESSIBILITY (ADA / EN 301 549 / BITV)
- Visual Impairment Support: Identification of <img> tags missing descriptive 'alt' attributes or using redundant placeholders.
- Semantic HTML Integrity: Basic audit of HTML structure to ensure compatibility with screen readers and assistive technologies.

4. E-COMMERCE INTEGRITY (SFCC SPECIALIZED)
- Third-Party Pixel Audit: Real-time monitoring of marketing pixel behavior and unauthorized third-party script injections.
- Transactional Flow Security: Identifying technical errors in critical checkout stages that could lead to data leakage or lost transactions.

LEGAL BASIS:
Audits are conducted under 'Legitimate Interest' (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). HumangoBot acts as a passive, stateless observer. No personal data is harvested or stored; only technical metadata and evidentiary artifacts are collected.

RETENTION POLICY:
Technical evidence (redacted snippets and low-res screenshots) is stored for 365 days for verification and audit trail purposes, followed by automated permanent deletion.
